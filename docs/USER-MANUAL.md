## 1. What is HisabKitab

HisabKitab is a bookkeeping and tax helper for small registered businesses in Nepal. You talk to it on WhatsApp, in Nepali, English, or Romanized Nepali (like "aaja ko sale 5000"). It understands your message, does the maths correctly, shows you what it understood, and saves it only after you agree.

Think of it as a careful junior accountant who works inside your WhatsApp, 24 hours a day, and never gets tired of small entries.

**What it does for you:**

- Records your sales and expenses from a text or a photo of a bill.
- Calculates VAT (13 percent) and TDS correctly, the Nepali way.
- Keeps track of who owes you money and who you owe.
- Prepares your monthly VAT return figures so you can file them.
- Sends you clean PDF reports you can forward to anyone.
- Reminds you before your VAT deadline so you never forget.

**What it never does:**

- It never guesses. If a bill is blurry or a number is unclear, it asks you.
- It never saves anything without your confirmation.
- It never files with the IRD or logs into any government portal. You always file yourself.
- It never asks for your passwords or OTPs. If you send one by mistake, it refuses and does not store it.

## 5. Step 2: Connect your WhatsApp (pairing)

Pairing links your WhatsApp number to your business so HisabKitab knows it is really you. You only do this once.

1. After you sign up, you receive a **pairing code** (a short code like `START 4821`).
2. Save HisabKitab's WhatsApp number to your contacts (you will get this number when you sign up).
3. Open WhatsApp and send this message to that number:

   > START 4821

   (Use your own code, not 4821.)

4. HisabKitab replies with a warm welcome and confirms your business is connected.

That is it. From now on, just message that number whenever you want to record something or ask a question.

> Safety note: only the phone number you paired can use your account. Nobody else can see your business data. One WhatsApp chat is for one business only.

## 7. Daily use: recording sales and expenses

This is the heart of HisabKitab. You tell it what happened, it shows you what it understood, and you confirm.

**Recording a sale**

You type:

> sold goods for 9000 today

HisabKitab replies with what it understood, for example: the amount, how much of that is VAT, and the date. It will ask:

> Shall I save this? Reply yes to confirm.

You reply:

> yes

Now it is saved. If something was wrong, you just tell it, for example "no, it was 9500", and it fixes it before saving.

**Recording an expense**

> paid 4500 for printing supplies

HisabKitab works out the VAT and TDS if they apply, shows you, and asks you to confirm. It will also tell you whether you can claim the input VAT credit or not, and why.

**Important idea: draft first, then confirm**

Every entry starts as a **draft**. Nothing is final until you say yes. This is on purpose, so a wrong number never sneaks into your books. You are always in control.

## 8. Sending a photo of a bill

You do not have to type long bills. Just take a photo.

1. Take a clear photo of the bill or invoice.
2. Send it to HisabKitab on WhatsApp.
3. It reads the bill and shows you each field it found: the vendor name, the date, the amount, the VAT, the invoice number, and more.
4. If anything is unclear, it points to that exact field and asks you to confirm or correct it. It never makes up a number it cannot read.
5. You confirm, and it saves.

If a photo is too blurry to read after two tries, HisabKitab will politely offer to let you type the details instead. You can also add a caption to your photo, for example:

> this is the electricity bill for the shop

That caption helps it understand the bill faster.

## 9. VAT and TDS made simple

You do not need to remember tax rules. HisabKitab knows them.

- **VAT** in Nepal is 13 percent. When you give an amount, HisabKitab tells you how much of it is VAT. If your amount already includes VAT, it splits it correctly. If it does not, it adds it. It always shows you which way it calculated, so there are no surprises.
- **TDS** is calculated on the amount before VAT, the correct way. HisabKitab applies the right rate and shows it.
- **Input VAT credit:** when you buy something, HisabKitab tells you whether you can claim the VAT back. For example, it will warn you if the vendor is not VAT registered, or if the bill is an abbreviated (small) bill, or if it is older than one year. These small rules are easy to miss, and HisabKitab catches them for you.

You can also just ask tax questions about your business, for example:

> how much VAT do I owe this month

HisabKitab answers using your real confirmed entries, never a guess.

## 10. Debtors and creditors (who owes you, who you owe)

*Available on Pro and Business.*

This is the feature most owners say pays for itself. Unpaid credit is where small businesses quietly lose the most money, simply because no one is tracking it. If you sell or buy on credit, this keeps every rupee straight. If you are on Starter and you sell on credit, this is the one reason to move up to Pro.

**Record a credit sale (someone owes you):**

> sold 20000 worth of goods to Ram on credit

**Record a credit purchase (you owe someone):**

> bought 15000 of stock from Sita Suppliers on credit, to pay later

**When a customer pays you:**

> Ram paid 10000 today

HisabKitab applies the payment to the oldest unpaid bill first, unless you tell it which invoice. It then shows the new balance.

**Ask anytime:**

> who owes me money
> how much does Ram still owe
> show me overdue payments
> did Sita Suppliers get paid

HisabKitab answers from your real records. It can also show you "aging", which means how long each amount has been unpaid (for example, 30 days, 60 days, 90 days). This helps you chase the right people first.

## 11. Collecting payments with Khalti

HisabKitab can help you collect a digital payment from your customer.

1. You say:

   > collect 5000 from a customer for catering

2. HisabKitab confirms the amount and reason, and asks for your "yes".
3. After you confirm, it creates a secure Khalti payment link.
4. You share that link with your customer on WhatsApp.
5. When the customer pays, HisabKitab checks with Khalti directly and only then records the sale. It never trusts a screenshot. This protects you from fake payment claims.

Refunds are handled the same careful way: they need your clear "yes" for that specific refund.

> eSewa and Fonepay are coming soon. For now, Khalti is the live option, and HisabKitab will offer it.

## 13. Monthly VAT return reminders

Around the 20th of each Nepali month, HisabKitab prepares your VAT return figures and shows you:

- your total sales,
- your output VAT,
- your input VAT,
- and the net amount payable,

all on one simple screen. It reminds you that even a "nil" return (a month with nothing to report) still has to be filed. You review the numbers, then file them yourself on the IRD portal. After you file, you tell HisabKitab "I filed it" and it marks it done.

HisabKitab also reminds you before the deadline so you are never caught late. When it tells you a deadline, it checks the official IRD calendar to confirm it, and if it cannot confirm, it says so honestly instead of guessing.

You can let trusted people help, with the right level of access.

To invite someone, you (the owner) send:

> add 9779812345678 as accountant

The roles are:

- **Owner:** that is you. Full control, including money actions and team changes.
- **Accountant:** can record and confirm entries, prepare VAT, and pull reports. Cannot move money or change the team.

## 16. What you can ask, and what is outside our area

HisabKitab is built to be your business accounts assistant. It is very flexible about anything to do with your money and books.

**Great things to ask (a real owner's daily questions):**

- How much did I sell this week or this month
- What were my biggest expenses
- How much VAT and TDS do I owe
- Who owes me money and who is overdue
- How much do I owe my suppliers
- Did a particular customer pay
- What is my profit picture this month (from your sales and expenses)
- Prepare my return, send me a statement, give me a report
- Remind me about my deadline

**What it will politely decline:**

HisabKitab stays focused on your business accounts. It will not answer general questions that are outside that area, for example:

- General knowledge, news, or weather
- Questions about public figures or politics
- Other businesses' data
- Jokes, stories, or chit chat
- Writing code or essays

If you ask one of these, it will not pretend to know. It will kindly say it is outside its area and remind you what it can help with. This is on purpose. A focused assistant that says "that is outside my area" is more trustworthy than one that guesses.

## 17. Managing your subscription and billing

- **Check your plan and usage:** ask "what is my usage this month" or "when does my subscription end".
- **Upgrade:** ask "upgrade my plan" and choose Pro or Business. You only pay the difference for the days left in your month, and every entry you already have stays exactly as it is. A good moment to move from Starter to Pro is the first time you ask "who owes me money" and realise you want that answer every day. A good moment to move from Pro to Business is the first time you need a clean PDF report for your bank, or you want your accountant working inside the system with you.
- **Renew:** you pay each month in advance. HisabKitab reminds you a few days before your month ends. Pay through the Khalti link to continue.
- **If you renew late:** you keep read access for a short grace period. After that, the account pauses, but your data is never deleted. As soon as you pay, everything comes back exactly as it was.
- **Cancel:** ask to cancel any time. You keep access until the end of the month you already paid for, and your data is retained.

Each plan has a fair monthly usage limit to keep the service fast for everyone. If you ever reach it, HisabKitab tells you clearly and nothing you sent is lost. You can wait for the next month or upgrade to continue right away.

## 18. Your data, privacy and safety

Your trust matters, so here is how we protect you, in plain words.

- **Your data is yours.** We use it only to run your bookkeeping. We never sell it and never use it for ads.
- **It is kept separate.** Your business data is isolated from every other business. Nobody else can see it.
- **Sensitive numbers are encrypted.** Your PAN and VAT numbers are stored in a scrambled, encrypted form, so even in the unlikely event of a breach they are protected.
- **Tamper proof records.** Your activity log is built so it cannot be secretly changed. The record of what happened stays honest.
- **No passwords in chat.** HisabKitab refuses passwords and OTPs by design and never stores them.
- **You can delete everything.** Ask us to delete your business data at any time, and we remove it fully and keep only a small proof that we honoured your request.

For full details, see our Terms, Privacy Policy, and Data Processing notice at https://hisabkitab.pro.

> Honest note: HisabKitab helps you keep records and prepare figures. It is assistance, not a substitute for a licensed auditor, and it does not provide statutory sign-off. You always review and file yourself.

## 19. Common questions

**Do I need to install an app?**
No. Everything happens inside WhatsApp.

**Which languages can I use?**
Nepali, English, and Romanized Nepali. Mix them freely.

**What if I make a typing mistake?**
Just tell HisabKitab the correct value before you confirm. Since nothing saves without your "yes", mistakes are easy to fix.

**Will it file my taxes for me?**
No, and that is intentional. It prepares the numbers and reminds you. You file on the IRD portal yourself. This keeps you in full control and compliant.

**Is my money safe when collecting payments?**
Yes. HisabKitab only records a payment after Khalti confirms it directly. Screenshots are never trusted.

**What happens if I forget to renew?**
You get reminders first. If the month lapses, you keep read access briefly, then the account pauses. Your data is safe and returns the moment you pay.

**Can my accountant use it too?**
Yes, on the Business plan you get an accountant seat, and on Pro you can add up to 3 people.

**What if a bill photo is unclear?**
HisabKitab tells you exactly which part it could not read and asks you. After two unclear photos, it offers manual entry.
